# /swiss-tax-construction — Swiss Tax Law for Construction and Real Estate

## Overview of Applicable Taxes

| Tax | Level | Who pays | Basis |
|-----|-------|---------|-------|
| IVA/TVA (VAT) | Federal | Business providing service | Invoice amount |
| Grundstückgewinnsteuer | Cantonal | Seller of real estate | Capital gain |
| Handänderungssteuer | Cantonal | Buyer + seller (usually split) | Transaction price |
| Liegenschaftssteuer | Cantonal/communal | Property owner | Fiscal value of property |
| Einkommenssteuer on rental income | Federal + cantonal | Owner | Net rental income |
| Mehrwertsteuer on developer | Cantonal (some) | Developer | Deemed gain from development |

---

## 1. IVA/TVA — VAT on Construction (MWSTG)

### Applicable Rates (from 1 January 2024)

| Rate | Category | Construction application |
|------|----------|-------------------------|
| **8.1%** (standard) | All normal goods and services | New construction, commercial renovation, architect fees, engineering |
| **2.6%** (reduced) | Specific categories | **Energy-saving measures on existing residential buildings** (MWSTG art. 25 para. 2 lit. a Ziff. 9) |
| **0%** (exempt) | Export | Cross-border services (rare in construction) |

### When the Reduced Rate (2.6%) Applies to Renovation
The reduced rate applies when **all three conditions** are met:
1. Existing building (not new construction)
2. **Residential use** (Wohngebäude / usage d'habitation) — commercial buildings: 8.1%
3. Work is an **energy measure**: insulation, window replacement, heating system, solar panels, heat pumps, façade renovation for thermal performance

**Mixed projects**: if a renovation combines energy measures (2.6%) and non-energy works (8.1%), invoices must be split by item or a reasonable allocation agreed with ESTV/AFC.

### VAT on Architect's Fees
- Always **8.1%** regardless of whether the project is new or renovation
- Architects registered for VAT must charge VAT on all invoices (MWSTG art. 10 — mandatory registration above CHF 100'000 annual revenue)
- VAT registration: ESTV (estv.admin.ch) — effective within 30 days of registration

### VAT on Land
- **Sale of bare land**: exempt from VAT (MWSTG art. 21 para. 2 Ziff. 20)
- **Sale of land with new building** (developer sale): taxable at 8.1% on the construction component; land portion remains exempt
- Option to tax (Option): parties may opt into VAT for otherwise exempt transactions if buyer is a VAT-registered company (recovers input VAT)

### Input VAT Recovery (Vorsteuerabzug)
- Businesses registered for VAT may recover input VAT on construction costs used for taxable activities
- Residential landlords: generally **no input VAT recovery** (rents are VAT-exempt unless opted in)
- Commercial landlords: full recovery if opted in; partial if mixed use

### ОСТРOW on Real Estate for VAT
Developers selling PPE units (condominium) or new residential buildings:
- Treated as a mixed transaction: land (exempt) + new building (taxable at 8.1%)
- Allocation method: construction cost / total price × purchase price = taxable base
- ESTV practice: use actual construction costs as proxy if total price not yet known

## 2. Grundstückgewinnsteuer (Capital Gains Tax on Real Estate)

### Overview
Cantonal tax on the **profit** from selling real property. Federal income tax does not apply to real estate gains (federal exemption for private capital gains on real property).

### Calculation
```
Selling price
– Acquisition cost (purchase price + notary fees + Grundbuch costs)
– Improvement costs (value-adding investments — NOT maintenance)
– Selling costs (notary, broker commission, advertising)
= TAXABLE GAIN
```

### Rates — General Structure (varies by canton)
Progressive rate based on **gain amount** AND **holding period**:
- Short holding period (< 5 years): **higher rate** (discourages speculation)
- Long holding period (> 20–30 years): **reduced rate** or flat rate (rewards long-term ownership)

| Holding period | Typical rate range (illustrative) |
|---------------|----------------------------------|
| < 1 year | 50–60% of gain |
| 2–5 years | 30–40% of gain |
| 5–10 years | 20–30% of gain |
| 10–20 years | 15–20% of gain |
| > 20 years | 10–15% of gain |

*Note: exact rates vary significantly by canton. Always verify with cantonal tax authority.*

### Exemption — Primary Residence Replacement (Eigenheim-Ersatz)
If seller reinvests **all proceeds** in an equivalent or more expensive primary residence within:
- **2 years** (most cantons) of the sale
Tax deferral (not exemption) — gain is "frozen" and will be taxed when the replacement property is eventually sold without replacement.

### Improvements vs. Maintenance: Critical Distinction

| Type | Tax treatment | Examples |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **Value-adding investments** (wertsteigernde Aufwendungen) | Deductible from gain (reduces Grundstückgewinnsteuer) | New floors replacing old, kitchen replacement, bathroom modernization, new annex |
| **Maintenance costs** (werterhaltende Aufwendungen) | Deductible from **income tax** (rental income) in the year incurred — NOT from capital gain | Repainting, replacing broken boiler like-for-like, repair of roof tiles |

**Architect's tip**: when assisting clients with renovation projects, distinguish and document both categories from the outset. Keep all invoices as evidence.

## 3. Income Tax Deductions for Property Owners

### Lump-Sum Deduction vs. Actual Costs
Swiss resident owners of rental property may choose annually:
- **Lump-sum deduction** (Pauschalabzug): 10% of gross rental income (properties < 10 years old) or 20% (≥ 10 years old) — covers all maintenance
- **Actual cost deduction**: all documented maintenance/value-preserving costs — better if major maintenance was performed

### Deductible Maintenance Items
- Repainting (interior and exterior)
- Roof repairs (not full replacement if that's a value-adding improvement)
- Heating system replacement (like-for-like)
- Plumbing repairs
- Window replacement (if necessary maintenance — if energy-driven upgrade: energy deduction applies)

### Energy-Saving Deductions (Energiesparende Massnahmen)
Federal and most cantonal tax laws allow **additional deduction** for energy-saving investments:
- Insulation, heat pumps, solar panels, controlled ventilation
- Deductible even if value-adding (exception to the normal rule)
- Can be spread over 3 tax years if amount is large (federal law)
- Documentation required: energy audit before/after, contractor invoices specifying energy measure

## 4. Liegenschaftssteuer (Property Tax)

Not all cantons levy this tax. Where it exists:
- **Basis**: cantonal fiscal value (Steuerwert) of the property — typically 70–80% of market value
- **Rate**: 0.5–2.0‰ per year (varies significantly)
- **Cantons with Liegenschaftssteuer**: TI (2‰), BE, VS, NE, JU, GE, FR
- **Cantons without**: ZH, LU, ZG, SH, SG (no separate property tax)
- Paid by the owner as of 1 January of the tax year

## 5. Handänderungssteuer (Transfer Tax) — Recap

See also `/swiss-real-estate-transaction`. Key rates:

| Canton | Rate | Who pays |
|--------|------|---------|
| ZH | 0% | — |
| BE | 1.8% | Usually split |
| TI | 2.0% | Buyer (custom: split) |
| GE | 3.0% | Usually split |
| VD | 2.2% | Buyer |
| VS | 2.0% | Usually split |

**Exemptions common across cantons**:
- Transfer between spouses or registered partners
- Inheritance (succession mortis causa)
- Transfers within a corporate group (in some cantons)
- Agricultural land subject to BGBB

## 6. Developer Project — Tax Planning Overview

For a developer building and selling PPE units:

```
1. Land purchase → Handänderungssteuer (rate by canton)
2. Construction phase → IVA/TVA 8.1% on all contractor + architect invoices
   (input VAT recoverable if developer is VAT-registered for sale of new buildings)
3. Sale of PPE units (new buildings) → IVA/TVA 8.1% on building component
   Land component → VAT exempt
4. Profit from development → NOT Grundstückgewinnsteuer (commercial developer)
   → ordinary corporate income tax (Gewinnsteuer AG/GmbH)
   or self-employment income tax (if individual trader)
5. Grundstückgewinnsteuer applies ONLY to private individuals selling real estate
   (or to companies specifically subject to cantonal property gain regimes)
```

## Quick Reference — Renovation Project Tax Checklist

For each renovation project, document the following for the client:

- [ ] Is the building residential or commercial? (affects VAT rate on energy works)
- [ ] List energy measures separately in contractor invoices (2.6% VAT)
- [ ] List non-energy works separately (8.1% VAT)
- [ ] Classify each cost item: value-adding vs. value-preserving
- [ ] File all invoices: needed for Grundstückgewinnsteuer deduction on future sale
- [ ] If owner rents: choose optimal deduction method (lump-sum vs. actual)
- [ ] Energy measures: are they deductible as Energiesparkosten on income tax?
- [ ] Assess Liegenschaftssteuer rate in the applicable canton
- [ ] If future sale planned within 5 years: warn client about high Grundstückgewinnsteuer rate on short holding periods
