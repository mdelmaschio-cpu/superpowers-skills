# /swiss-property-law — Swiss Property Law (ZGB Sachenrecht)

## Legal Basis

**ZGB** (Zivilgesetzbuch / Code civil suisse) — Swiss Civil Code, Book IV (art. 641–977).
Property rights in Switzerland are governed exclusively by federal law; cantons administer the Grundbuch registry.

---

## 1. Grundbuch (Land Registry — ZGB art. 942–977)

### Structure of a Grundbuch Extract

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Bestandteil** | Plot description, area, cantonal parcel number (Parzellennummer) |
| **Eigentum** | Owner(s), acquisition date and legal basis |
| **Dienstbarkeiten** | Easements burdening or benefiting the plot |
| **Grundlasten** | Real burdens (Reallasten) — recurring obligations attached to land |
| **Grundpfandrechte** | Mortgages (Schuldbrief, Grundpfandverschreibung) |

### Key Points
- Entry in the Grundbuch is **constitutive** for property transfers — title passes at inscription, not at contract signing
- Public faith principle (öffentlicher Glaube, ZGB art. 973): third parties acquiring in good faith reliance on the register are protected
- **ÖREB/RDPPF** (Öffentlich-rechtliche Eigentumsbeschränkungen): public law restrictions (building zones, protected areas, contamination) are shown in the cantonal ÖREB cadastre — separate from the Grundbuch but equally important for due diligence

### Grundbuch Extract Types
- **Vollständiger Auszug**: all entries — required for notarial purchase deed
- **Beglaubigter Auszug**: certified copy — admissible as evidence
- Online access: varies by canton (ZH: grundbuchamt.ch; TI: ti.ch/dt; GE: ge.ch)

## 2. Baurecht / Superficie (ZGB art. 779–779l)

### What It Is
A registered right to build on and use another person's land for a defined period. The building becomes an accessory to the right, not to the land — it belongs to the holder of the Baurecht (Bauberechtigter), not to the landowner.

### Key Terms
| Parameter | Typical range |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Duration** | 30–100 years (max 100 years, renewable) |
| **Baurechtszins** | Annual canon: 4–6% of land value (negotiated) |
| **Reversion (Heimfall)** | At expiry: building reverts to landowner; compensation negotiated (often 50–100% of residual value) |
| **Form** | Notarial deed + inscription in Grundbuch as a distinct right (selbständiges und dauerndes Recht) |

### Relevance for Architects
- Common in affordable housing, public/private development, cooperative housing (Genossenschaft)
- Check Heimfall date: if < 30 years remaining, financing is difficult; affects project feasibility
- Building permits are issued to the Bauberechtigter (right holder), not the landowner
- Modifications to the building may require landowner consent — check Baurechtsvertrag

## 3. Stockwerkeigentum / PPE (ZGB art. 712a–712t)

### Definition
Fractional co-ownership of land with an exclusive right to use a defined unit (apartment, commercial space). Each unit = a separate Grundbuch entry with its own parcel number suffix (e.g., Parzelle 1234-A).

### Structure
```
Land co-ownership (Miteigentumsanteil)
    └── Exclusive right of use (Sonderrecht) — the apartment/unit
    └── Gemeinsame Teile (common areas) — shared by all owners
```

### Reglement (Condominium Rules — ZGB art. 712g)
Must be registered in the Grundbuch. Covers:
- Cost allocation keys (Wertquoten) for common expenses
- Use restrictions on individual units
- Administration (Verwalter appointment, assembly voting thresholds)
- Renovation fund (Erneuerungsfonds — not mandatory but standard practice)

### Architect's Tasks in PPE Projects
- Define the Sonderrechtsbeschreibung (boundary of each unit) — requires precise drawings
- Assign Wertquoten (value shares) in thousandths — based on floor area or negotiated
- These form part of the notarial constitutive deed and Grundbuch inscription
- Any structural change to a unit affecting common elements requires assembly approval

## 4. Dienstbarkeiten (Easements — ZGB art. 730–781)

### Types Relevant to Architecture

| Type | Content | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Wegrecht** | Right of way (pedestrian, vehicle) | Access across neighbor's plot |
| **Näherbaurecht** | Right to build closer than cantonal setback | Agreed reduction of Grenzabstand |
| **Leitungsrecht** | Right to lay pipes/cables | Sewer line crossing third plot |
| **Aussichtsservitut** | View protection — prohibits building above defined height | Alpine/lake view protection |
| **Quellenrecht** | Spring water right | Mountain spring serving a building |
| **Bauverbot** | Building prohibition on a plot | Green buffer zone |

### Key Rule
Easements run with the land — they bind all future owners automatically. A buyer cannot eliminate an easement unilaterally. Always check Grundbuch Dienstbarkeiten before designing — they may restrict height, setbacks, or use.

### Creation/Deletion
- Created by notarial deed + Grundbuch inscription
- Deleted by agreement of both dominant and servient owners + notarial deed + cancellation from register

## 5. Nachbarrecht (Neighbor Law — ZGB art. 684–698)

### Immissions (art. 684)
Owners must refrain from **excessive** disturbance of neighbors from: noise, smoke, vibration, smell, light, heat. Threshold: what is customary in the locality and what cannot be avoided by reasonable use of the property.

**Construction site**: noise, dust, and vibration from construction are generally tolerated if within cantonal norms (BAFU/FOEN noise limits); excessive or unreasonable disturbance may trigger civil claim.

### Setback Distances (art. 686)
Federal law delegates to cantons. Each cantonal Einführungsgesetz ZGB or Baugesetz specifies:
- **Grenzabstand** (setback from boundary): typically 3–4m for buildings above 3m height; check cantonal Baureglement
- Reduction by easement (Näherbaurecht) or mutual agreement + inscription

### Overhanging Branches and Encroachments (art. 687–689)
- Neighbor may cut overhanging branches if they cause damage (after notice)
- Roots or branches encroaching — reasonable notice required before cutting
- Building encroachments (Übergriff): ZGB art. 674 — if in good faith, court may grant rights but must compensate

### Action for Excessive Immissions
- Injunction (Unterlassungsklage) + damages (OR art. 41)
- Expert assessment of noise levels often required (cantonal Lärmschutzverordnung)
- Typically: cantonal civil court → Federal Supreme Court on appeal

## 6. Grundpfandrechte (Mortgages — ZGB art. 793–883)

### Schuldbrief (Bearer Mortgage Certificate — art. 842)
Most common form since 2012 reform. Exists as:
- **Paper Schuldbrief**: physical document, pledged to bank
- **Register-Schuldbrief**: purely electronic in Grundbuch (now standard)

Key feature: the Schuldbrief is independent of the underlying debt — it can be reused for new loans without Grundbuch amendment (just internal bank arrangement). Maximum mortgage amount is registered, not the actual loan balance.

### Priority (Pfandstellen)
Mortgages have priority by registration date (erste, zweite, dritte Stelle). Banks typically lend:
- 1st position (Erstpfand): up to 66–80% of property value
- 2nd position (Zweithypothek): up to 80–90%, higher interest rate, amortization required

### Relevance for Projects
- Project financing: construction loan (Baukredit) drawn in tranches as construction progresses
- Grundpfandrecht inscribed before construction — prerequisite for Baukredit
- Releases (Entlassung) of plots from mortgage: needed when selling individual PPE units in a development

## Due Diligence Checklist (Before Designing a Project)

- [ ] Obtain certified Grundbuch extract (all sections)
- [ ] Check ÖREB/RDPPF cadastre for public law restrictions
- [ ] Identify all Dienstbarkeiten — map their effect on the site
- [ ] Verify Baurecht duration if site is held under superficie — check Heimfall date
- [ ] Check Stockwerkeigentum Reglement if working on PPE renovation — approval thresholds
- [ ] Verify cantonal Grenzabstand and any Näherbaurecht easements reducing setbacks
- [ ] Check Grundpfandrechte: any mortgage that might require lender consent for modifications
- [ ] Identify neighbor parcels — any Aussichtsservitut or Bauverbot affecting design height?
