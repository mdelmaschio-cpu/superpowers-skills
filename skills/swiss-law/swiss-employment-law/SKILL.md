# /swiss-employment-law — Swiss Employment Law

## Legal Basis

- **OR art. 319–362**: Individual employment contract (Einzelarbeitsvertrag / contrat individuel de travail)
- **ArG** (Arbeitsgesetz): maximum hours, health protection, special categories
- **GAV/CCT** (Gesamtarbeitsvertrag / convention collective de travail): sector collective agreements — override OR minimums in favor of employees
- **AVG** (Arbeitsvermittlungsgesetz): temp agencies

## Employment Contract (Arbeitsvertrag)

### Required in Writing (not mandatory but strongly recommended)
OR art. 330b: employer must confirm key terms in writing within 1 month:
- Name of parties
- Start date
- Function/role
- Salary and components
- Working hours
- Notice period

### Duration
- **Indefinite** (unbefristet): standard; terminated by notice
- **Fixed-term** (befristet): ends automatically; no notice needed; cannot be repeatedly renewed to avoid indefinite contract protection

## Working Hours

| Category | Maximum hours/week |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Industrial/office workers** | 45 hours (ArG art. 9) |
| Other workers | 50 hours |
| Management / senior staff | No statutory limit (ArG art. 3 lit. d) — but or 362 still applies |

**Overtime (Überzeit)**: hours beyond maximum legal/contractual → entitled to 25% wage supplement OR compensatory time off by agreement.

**Trust-based hours (Vertrauensarbeitszeit)**: common in architecture — employees manage own hours, no overtime claims. Must be explicitly agreed; applies to staff with significant autonomy.

## Salary

- No federal statutory minimum wage (except cantonal minimums: GE, TI, NE, JU, BS, BL, SO — check current amounts)
- **13th month salary**: not legally required but common in CH — if paid historically, becomes contractual entitlement
- **Bonus/variable**: discretionary unless contractually committed
- Social contributions (employer share):
  - AHV/IV/EO: ~5.3% of gross
  - ALV (unemployment): ~1.1%
  - BVG (pension): variable, at least 50% of total contribution
  - SUVA (accident insurance): ~0.13–3% (work accident) + optional non-work accident

## Notice Periods (OR art. 335)

| Probation | Notice |
|-----------|--------|
| During probation (max 3 months) | 7 days |
| **Year 1** | **1 month**, end of month |
| **Years 2–9** | **2 months**, end of month |
| **Year 10+** | **3 months**, end of month |

Can be extended by contract (not shortened below statutory).

## Termination

### Ordinary Termination (ordentliche Kündigung)
- Either party, with notice period
- No reason required (but abuse rules apply)
- Employer must give reason in writing if employee requests (OR art. 335)

### Wrongful Dismissal (missbräuchliche Kündigung — OR art. 336)
Employer liable for up to 6 months' salary if terminates:
- Because employee exercised legal rights
- To prevent entitlement accruing (e.g., just before pension entitlement)
- For discrimination reasons
- Without genuinely considering employee interests

### Protected Periods — No Termination by Employer (OR art. 336c)
- Illness/accident: 30 days (year 1), 90 days (years 2–5), 180 days (year 6+)
- Military service
- Pregnancy + 16 weeks post-birth
- Adoption leave

If notice given during protected period: suspended and resumes after.

### Summary Dismissal (fristlose Kündigung — OR art. 337)
Immediate termination for cause — requires serious breach:
- Theft from employer
- Serious insubordination
- Falsification of documents
- Repeated and warned conduct

Must act within 2–3 working days of discovering cause.

## Non-Compete Clause (Konkurrenzverbot — OR art. 340)

Valid only if:
- Employee has access to clients or business secrets (not just any employee)
- Duration: maximum **3 years** (court often reduces to 1–2 years)
- Geographic scope: must be reasonable
- Scope of activity: precisely defined
- Compensation: OR does not require, but lack of compensation is factor in reasonableness

Employer may waive by paying agreed indemnity. Employee breach: employer may claim actual damages (difficult to prove) or agreed penalty (Konventionalstrafe).

## Collective Agreement — Architecture Sector

**GAV Architektur- und Ingenieurbüros** (regional — not all cantons):
Check with cantonal SIA section and VSA (Verband Schweizerischer Architektinnen und Architekten) whether your canton has a binding GAV.

Where applicable, GAV sets minimums for:
- Minimum salaries by qualification level
- Maximum overtime thresholds
- Vacation entitlement (typically 25 days for professionals)
- Training and development contributions

## Vacation (Ferien — OR art. 329a)

| Age / seniority | Minimum days/year |
|----------------|------------------|
| Under 20 years old | **5 weeks (25 days)** |
| All others | **4 weeks (20 days)** |

Many architecture employers voluntarily give 25 days. GAV may require more.

## Leave Entitlements

| Leave type | Duration | Legal basis |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Maternity | 14 weeks at 80% salary | EOG / LAPG |
| Paternity | 2 weeks at 80% salary | EOG (since 2021) |
| Adoption | 2 weeks (if child <4 years) | EOG (since 2023) |
| Bereavement | 3 days (immediate family) | OR art. 329 |
| Marriage | Typically 1–3 days | By agreement |
| Military service | Salary continued (EO compensation) | EOG |

## Termination Checklist (Employer)

- [ ] Verify notice period (contract and OR)
- [ ] Check protected period (illness/pregnancy) — suspension applies
- [ ] Deliver notice in writing (registered mail recommended)
- [ ] Offer exit interview and document
- [ ] Calculate outstanding salary, vacation, 13th month pro-rata
- [ ] Prepare reference letter (Arbeitszeugnis) — mandatory on request, must be truthful and benevolent
- [ ] Handle social insurance notifications (AHV, BVG, SUVA)
- [ ] Return of company equipment and access revocation

## Reference Letter (Arbeitszeugnis — OR art. 330a)

- **Mandatory** on request; must be truthful, complete, and benevolent
- Cannot contain coded negative language (case law prohibits)
- Employee may sue for correction if defamatory or inaccurate
- Standard elements: duration, function, tasks, performance, conduct, reason for leaving (optional), closing phrase
