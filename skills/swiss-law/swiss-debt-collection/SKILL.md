# /swiss-debt-collection — Swiss Debt Collection (SchKG)

The **SchKG** (Schuldbetreibungs- und Konkursgesetz / loi sur la poursuite pour dettes et la faillite) governs all debt enforcement in Switzerland. The system is administered by cantonal **Betreibungsämter** (debt enforcement offices).

## Overview of the Process

```
Unpaid invoice
      ↓
  Zahlungsbefehl (payment order) — from Betreibungsamt
      ↓
  [No opposition within 10 days]     [Opposition: Rechtsvorschlag filed]
      ↓                                        ↓
  Continue Betreibung                 Rigetto dell'opposizione needed
      ↓                                        ↓
  Pfändung (seizure of assets)       Court action to lift opposition
  or Konkurs (bankruptcy — for          ↓
  registered companies)              Continue if won
```

## Step 1 — Betreibungsbegehren (Initiate Enforcement)

**File at**: Betreibungsamt of debtor's domicile (for individuals) or registered office (for companies)

**Cost**: CHF 15–50 (scales with debt amount)

**What to provide**:
- Debtor's full name and address
- Debt amount (CHF)
- Brief description of claim ("Honorar Architekturleistungen gemäss Rechnung Nr. XX vom [date]")
- Your bank account for payment

**Online filing**: many cantons allow online via betreibung.ch or cantonal portal.

The Betreibungsamt issues a **Zahlungsbefehl** within a few days, sent to debtor by registered mail.

## Step 2 — Rechtsvorschlag (Opposition)

Debtor has **10 days** from receipt of Zahlungsbefehl to file a Rechtsvorschlag (simple written statement). This **stops the enforcement** — debtor does not need to give reasons.

**If no Rechtsvorschlag**: proceed directly to Step 4 (continuation).

**If Rechtsvorschlag filed**: you must have the opposition lifted by court before proceeding.

## Step 3 — Lifting the Opposition (Beseitigung des Rechtsvorschlags)

### Option A: Rigetto Provvisorio (provisional lifting — SchKG art. 82)

**Fast, no full trial needed. Requires**: a written, signed document by debtor acknowledging the debt (e.g., signed contract, signed invoice, written promise to pay, email confirming the debt amount).

- File petition (Gesuch) at cantonal court
- Court reviews documents — no hearing in most cases
- If granted: enforcement continues; debtor must then sue to annul within 20 days

**Practical for architects**: a signed mandate contract + signed invoice = strong basis for rigetto provvisorio.

### Option B: Rigetto Definitivo (definitive lifting — SchKG art. 80–81)

**Required when**: you have a final court judgment, arbitration award, or notarized deed.

- File petition at court with judgment/award
- Court grants without examination of merits

### Option C: Ordinary Action (SchKG art. 83 / 79)

If no written document exists: file a full claim (Anerkennungsklage) in cantonal court:
- Prove the debt through evidence (emails, work records, time sheets)
- Court issues judgment → use for rigetto definitivo
- Slower (months to years) and more expensive

## Step 4 — Continuation (Fortsetzungsbegehren)

File within **1 year** of Zahlungsbefehl (or after opposition lifted). Request continuation at Betreibungsamt.

For **individual debtors**: leads to Pfändung (asset seizure).
For **registered companies** (AG, GmbH): leads to Konkursandrohung (bankruptcy threat).

## Step 5a — Pfändung (Asset Seizure — for individuals)

Betreibungsamt:
1. Schedules appointment with debtor to inventory assets
2. Seizes: bank accounts, salary (above subsistence), real property, vehicles, valuables
3. **Protected from seizure**: tools of trade up to CHF ~1'000, basic household goods, subsistence income
4. Sells seized assets at auction (Verwertung)
5. Proceeds distributed to creditors

Timeline from filing to receipt of money: typically 6–18 months.

## Step 5b — Konkurs (Bankruptcy — for companies)

1. **Konkursandrohung**: formal warning issued; debtor has 20 days to pay
2. **Konkursbegehren**: if unpaid, creditor requests bankruptcy proceedings
3. Court opens bankruptcy → liquidation
4. Betreibungsamt notifies all creditors (public notice)
5. File your claim (Konkurseingabe) within set deadline
6. Distribution after liquidation: secured creditors first, then by privilege class

**Practical reality**: if company enters bankruptcy, unsecured creditors (ordinary class — third privilege) often recover little or nothing. Prioritize speed over thoroughness.

## Privilege Classes in Bankruptcy

| Class | Creditors | Recovery likelihood |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Secured** | Mortgage creditors, pledges | High |
| **1st privilege** | Employee wages (last 6 months), AHV contributions | High |
| **2nd privilege** | Cantonal tax, some pension claims | Medium |
| **3rd privilege** | All other creditors (including architect's fees) | Low–zero |

## Betreibungsregisterauszug (Debt Collection Extract)

Any person with legitimate interest can request a debtor's enforcement history from the Betreibungsamt. Architects often request this before accepting a large commission from an unknown client.

**Costs**: CHF 17–35
**Shows**: active Betreibungen, Pfändungen, and any past bankruptcies for 5 years

## Practical Strategy for Unpaid Architect's Fees

### Before Filing

1. Send a formal written **Mahnung** (demand letter) by registered mail
2. Set a 10-day deadline
3. Offer payment plan if client has cash flow issues — better than collection
4. Check debtor's Betreibungsregister before escalating

### Filing Decision

| Situation | Recommended action |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Individual, likely has assets | Betreibung → Pfändung path |
| Company, healthy balance sheet | Betreibung → Konkurs threat (usually sufficient) |
| Company, may be insolvent | Act fast — priority over other creditors |
| Disputed claim, no signed documents | Consider negotiation first; litigation is slow |

### Limitation Periods (Verjährung)

Architect's fees (Honorar): **5 years** from when the fee was due (OR art. 128). After 5 years, claim is time-barred. Filing a Betreibung interrupts the limitation period.
