# /swiss-tenancy-law — Swiss Tenancy Law (Mietrecht / Droit du bail)

## Legal Basis

- **OR art. 253–304**: mandatory federal tenancy law
- **VMWG** (Verordnung über die Miete): implementing ordinance
- Key principle: **tenant protection** — many provisions favor tenants and cannot be contractually waived

## Lease Agreement (Mietvertrag)

### Essential Elements
- Description of premises
- Start date and duration (fixed or indefinite)
- Rent amount (Nettomietzins + Nebenkosten/Heizung)
- Notice period

### Advance Rent / Deposit (Kaution)
- Maximum: **3 months' net rent**
- Must be deposited in tenant's name at a Swiss bank (blocked account)
- Released: by agreement or court order after end of tenancy
- Landlord cannot keep it unilaterally for alleged damages

## Rent

### Components
- **Nettomietzins**: base rent
- **Nebenkosten (NK)**: ancillary costs (heating, hot water, janitor) — must be specified and documented
- **Gross rent (Bruttomiete)**: all-inclusive (simpler but less transparent)

### Rent Increases — Validity Requirements
Rent increases require **official form** (amtliches Formular) — violation = void:

Valid grounds for increase:
- Rise in mortgage reference interest rate (Referenzzinssatz): +0.25% rate = max ~2–3% increase
- Inflation: 40% of CPI increase since last adjustment
- Value-adding improvements (wertsteigernde Investitionen): pro-rata on cost
- Returns below standard if initial rent set below market

**Tenant's rights**: contest within 30 days to Schlichtungsbehörde

### Mortgage Reference Interest Rate (2024)
- Current rate: 1.75% (verify at admin.ch/referenzzinssatz — changes quarterly)
- Below 2%: lowest tier; triggers reduction rights if rent historically high

## Notice Periods

### Residential Tenancies

| Who gives notice | Period | Timing |
|-----------------|--------|--------|
| Landlord | **3 months** | By end of month; local term dates (Ortsgebrauch) |
| Tenant | **3 months** | By end of month; local term dates |
| Fixed-term lease | — | No ordinary notice; terminates at agreed date |

Local term dates (Zürcher Usanz example): 31 March, 30 June, 30 September, 31 December

### Commercial Tenancies
- Notice: **6 months** (default) unless contracted otherwise
- More flexible than residential — can agree shorter/longer periods
- But: abuse of notice rights still applies

## Termination

### Ordinary Termination
- Must use official form (amtliches Formular) for landlord — otherwise void
- Tenant: written notice sufficient
- Tenant may contest **abusive termination** (missbräuchliche Kündigung) within 30 days

**Abusive termination examples (OR art. 271):**
- Landlord terminates because tenant exercised legal rights (complained to authority, contested rent)
- Termination to enable rent increase to new tenant
- Termination disproportionate to reason given

### Extraordinary Termination
- Both parties: for material breach (unzumutbare Verhältnisse) with immediate effect after written warning
- Tenant: if landlord fails to remedy serious defects after notification

## Defects in Rental Property

### Tenant's Rights (OR art. 259)
- **Minor defects**: tenant remedies at own cost (or bears them)
- **Significant defects**: notify landlord in writing → landlord has reasonable period to remedy
- **Major defects** (inhabitable): tenant may terminate immediately or withhold rent

### Rent Reduction (Mietzinsreduktion)
- Tenant may reduce rent proportionate to diminished utility during defect period
- Retroactive: from date of notification to landlord
- Process: notify → Schlichtungsbehörde if landlord disputes

### Withholding Rent (OR art. 259g)
- Tenant may pay into blocked account (consignation) rather than to landlord
- Purpose: pressure landlord to remedy; money released when defect fixed or by court order
- Not the same as not paying rent — must follow procedure

## Dispute Resolution

### 1. Schlichtungsbehörde (Conciliation Authority)
- **Mandatory first step** for all tenancy disputes in Switzerland
- Free of charge for tenants
- Local authority (Mietgericht, Commission de conciliation)
- Conciliation hearing: informal, parties try to agree
- If no agreement: Autorisation to proceed to court issued

### 2. Cantonal Court (Mietgericht)
- After failed conciliation
- Simplified procedure for claims ≤ CHF 30'000

### 3. Appeal
- Cantonal appeal court → Federal Supreme Court (on legal questions only)

## Landlord's Rights at End of Tenancy

- **Handover inspection** (Abnahmeprotokoll): document condition jointly
- Damage beyond normal wear and tear: deduct from deposit
- **Normal wear and tear** (normaler Verschleiß): landlord bears; tenant does NOT pay for
- Repainting: only if lease term was long enough to require it (10-year table)
- Keys: all copies returned

## Practical Checklists

### Entering a Tenancy
- [ ] Check rent against area averages (miete.ch)
- [ ] Document move-in condition in writing (Übergabeprotokoll) with photos
- [ ] Open separate blocked account for deposit — do not pay cash to landlord
- [ ] Read Nebenkosten provisions carefully

### Contesting Rent Increase
- [ ] Receive official form — if no form, increase is void
- [ ] Calculate whether basis (reference interest rate / inflation) justifies the amount
- [ ] File objection within **30 days** at Schlichtungsbehörde
