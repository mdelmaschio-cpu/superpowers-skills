# /swiss-construction-law — Swiss Construction Law

## Legal Sources (Hierarchy)

1. **Swiss Code of Obligations (OR/CO) art. 363–379** — mandatory statutory framework for Werkvertrag
2. **SIA 118** — standard conditions, apply if incorporated by contract
3. **Special conditions** (Besondere Bedingungen) — project-specific, override SIA 118
4. **Cantonal building law** — permits, safety, construction procedure

## Werkvertrag (OR art. 363) — Contract for Work

### Key Characteristics
- Contractor owes a **result** (Werk), not just best efforts (unlike Auftrag)
- Price: fixed (Pauschalpreis), unit rates (Einheitspreise), or cost-plus
- Acceptance (Abnahme): moment of transfer of risk and start of defect period

### Contractor's Obligations
- Produce the agreed work in accordance with contract and rules of the art
- Notify client of impediments discovered during execution (OR art. 365)
- Warranty for defects: concealment = fraud (10-year liability)

### Client's Obligations
- Accept the work when ready (cannot unreasonably refuse)
- Pay the agreed price
- Cooperate (provide access, decisions, approvals)

## Defect Liability — Detailed

### Discovery at Abnahme (OR art. 367–368)
- **Obvious defects** (erkennbare Mängel): must be reserved in writing at Abnahme or lost
- **Hidden defects** (verborgene Mängel): must be notified promptly upon discovery

### Limitation Periods (OR art. 371)

| Defect type | Period | Cannot be shortened? |
|-------------|--------|----------------------|
| Movable work | 2 years | No (can be extended) |
| **Immovable work (buildings)** | **5 years** | **Yes — mandatory (OR art. 371 §3)** |
| Fraudulent concealment | 10 years | Yes |
| SIA 118 §180 | 2 years | Yes — but CO 5-year applies additionally |

**Critical**: For buildings, the 5-year CO period cannot be contractually reduced. SIA 118's 2-year period coexists — the client benefits from whichever is longer for each defect type.

### Remedies (OR art. 368)
1. **Repair** (Nachbesserung): client may demand at contractor's cost
2. **Price reduction** (Minderung): proportionate to defect's impact on value
3. **Rescission** (Wandelung): only if defect is so grave the work is useless
4. **Damages** (Schadenersatz): if contractor at fault (OR art. 97)

### Architect's Warranty to Client
- Architect is liable for defects in their **plans and supervision** (OR art. 363 applied to mandate)
- Limitation: 10 years from completion of work (OR art. 127)
- Professional liability insurance (Berufshaftpflicht) required under SIA 102

## Construction Insurance

### Mandatory / Standard Policies

| Insurance | Who takes | What it covers |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Bauwesenversicherung** (all-risk construction) | Contractor or client | Damage to the works during construction |
| **Haftpflichtversicherung** (liability) | All parties | Third-party bodily injury / property damage |
| **Berufshaftpflicht** (professional liability) | Architect/engineer | Design and supervision errors |
| **Gebäudeversicherung** (cantonal) | Building owner | Fire, natural hazards — mandatory in most cantons |

### Claims Process
- Notify insurer within contractual period (typically 7–30 days of incident)
- Document damage with photos, expert report
- Do not repair before insurer inspection (unless safety emergency)
- Subrogation: insurer steps into policyholder's rights vs. responsible party

## Disputes — Resolution Path

### 1. Negotiation
- Document all communications in writing
- Reference specific contractual provisions and Swiss law articles
- Issue formal Mängelrüge or Mahnung (demand letter)

### 2. Expert Determination (SIA 118 §96)
- Either party may request appointment of neutral expert
- Expert determines technical questions (defect existence, quantum)
- Result binding unless challenged in court within 30 days

### 3. Mediation
- Recommended before litigation; faster and cheaper
- Not mandatory unless contractually agreed

### 4. Cantonal Court
- Jurisdiction: cantonal Handelsgericht (commercial court) for commercial disputes
- Value threshold: varies by canton (ZH: CHF 30'000)
- Simplified procedure: claims ≤ CHF 30'000

### 5. Federal Supreme Court
- Appeal on questions of federal law (OR)
- Limited review of facts

## Contractor Insolvency During Construction

- **Priority of architect**: immediately secure outstanding invoices and drawings
- **Client's rights**: terminate contract; take possession of materials on site
- **Unfinished work**: document precisely; commission new contractor
- **Retention**: may be set off against completion costs
- **Bankruptcy proceedings**: report claim to liquidator (Konkursmasse)
