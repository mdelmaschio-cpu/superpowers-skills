# /swiss-ip-architects — Intellectual Property for Architects (Swiss URG)

## Legal Basis

**URG** (Urheberrechtsgesetz / Loi sur le droit d'auteur) — Swiss Federal Act on Copyright, last revised 2020.

Key principle: copyright arises **automatically** at the moment of creation — no registration, no deposit, no © symbol required.

## What Is Protected

### Architectural Works (URG art. 2)

| Protected | Not protected |
|-----------|--------------|
| Architectural designs with individual character (Individualität) | Pure technical drawings without creative choices |
| Physical buildings with creative design | Purely functional, standardized structures |
| Urban planning schemes with original character | Standard elements (windows, door shapes by themselves) |
| Scale models | Building materials and construction systems |
| Interior design layouts if original | Copying inspired by a work (if independently created) |

**"Individual character"** (Individualität): the threshold in Switzerland is low — any creative choice that reflects the author's personality qualifies. Most professional architectural designs are protected.

### What Does Not Require Originality
- Plans and drawings are also protected as **graphic works** regardless of the building's creative level

## Who Owns the Copyright

### General Rule (URG art. 6)
**The architect** (natural person who created the work) owns the copyright — **not the client**, not the firm.

### Employment (URG art. 17)
When an architect creates a work **in the course of employment**:
- The **employer** acquires the right to use the work for the purposes of the employment
- The **employee retains copyright ownership** (moral rights cannot be transferred)
- Practical effect: firm can use the design; architect retains attribution rights

### Multiple Authors (URG art. 7)
When several architects contribute: **joint authorship** — all co-authors must agree to any use or license.

## Moral Rights (Urheberpersönlichkeitsrechte — URG art. 9–11)

**Cannot be transferred or waived** — unlike economic rights.

| Moral right | Content |
|-------------|---------|
| **Right of disclosure** | Architect decides whether/when to publish the work |
| **Right of attribution** | Architect's name must be mentioned on the building, publications, competitions |
| **Right of integrity** | No modifications that harm the architect's reputation or honor |

### Right of Integrity in Practice
- Client modifies the building after completion without architect's consent → potential URG violation
- **Significant modifications** (change of façade, addition of floors, demolition) may infringe integrity right
- Architect can object; court may order removal of modifications or award damages
- **Demolition**: controversial — some Swiss courts hold architect has no right to prevent demolition of own building

### Attribution on Buildings
- Architect's name must appear on any published reproduction of the building (photos, brochures)
- Competition entries: organizer must credit winning architect
- Attribution cannot be contractually waived (but can be informally ignored in practice — URG enforcement needed)

## Economic Rights (Verwertungsrechte — URG art. 10)

Rights the architect controls and can transfer/license:

| Right | Covers |
|-------|--------|
| **Reproduction** | Copying plans, making prints, digital reproduction |
| **Distribution** | Selling copies of plans |
| **Communication** | Publishing online, in books, exhibitions |
| **Building** | Constructing the building from the plans |
| **Modification** | Creating derivative works, adaptations |

### Transfer of Economic Rights to Client

Common in mandate contracts — typical clause:

> "Der Auftraggeber erhält das nicht-ausschliessliche Recht, die im Rahmen dieses Mandats erstellten Pläne und Zeichnungen für den Bau des Objekts an der vereinbarten Adresse zu verwenden. Jede andere Verwendung bedarf der schriftlichen Zustimmung des Architekten."
>
> *(The client receives the non-exclusive right to use the plans and drawings created under this mandate for the construction of the building at the agreed address. Any other use requires the written consent of the architect.)*

**Critical distinction**:
- Client can build **the agreed building** from the plans → licensed by mandate
- Client **cannot**: copy the design for another building, publish plans, sell to a developer

### What Happens When Mandate Ends Without Completion

If client terminates early:
- Right to use plans **for that specific building** typically survives — unless contract says otherwise
- Client **cannot** reuse plans with another architect without license
- Unbuilt designs remain architect's intellectual property

## Licensing

Licenses may be:
- **Exclusive** (ausschliesslich): only licensee may use; even architect cannot license to others
- **Non-exclusive** (nicht-ausschliesslich): architect may grant same rights to multiple parties
- **Territorial** / **temporal** limits possible

### License Fees (Urheberrechtsvergütung)
When a client reuses plans for a second building, a phase II of the same project, or permits another use: architect is entitled to a separate license fee, typically calculated as a percentage of the SIA 102 fee that would have applied.

## Infringement — What Constitutes a Violation

| Situation | Violation? |
|-----------|-----------|
| Client builds from plans without paying final invoice | Probably yes — right to use may be conditional on payment |
| Developer copies façade design from published photo | Yes — reproduction + building right infringed |
| Contractor modifies design during construction without consent | Depends on extent; significant changes = integrity violation |
| Another architect independently creates similar design | No — must be proven copied, not independently created |
| Client photographs building for own use | No — URG art. 27: buildings visible from public space may be freely photographed |

### Photography Exception (URG art. 27)
Buildings visible from public streets or spaces may be freely photographed and the images used — including commercially. This is the **Panoramafreiheit** (panorama exception). Architect cannot prevent this.

## Enforcement

### Civil (URG art. 62–67)
- **Injunction** (superprovisorische Verfügung): emergency stop to ongoing infringement
- **Damages**: actual loss + lost profits
- **Account of profits**: infringer must hand over profits made from violation
- **Moral damages** (Genugtuung): for non-economic harm to reputation
- Cantonal civil court; Federal Supreme Court on appeal

### Criminal (URG art. 67–69)
- Willful infringement: up to 3 years imprisonment or fine
- Prosecution: on complaint (Antragsdelikt)
- Rarely pursued in civil architecture disputes; civil action more practical

## Practical Checklist for Mandate Contracts

Include in every Mandatsvertrag:

- [ ] Architect retains copyright; client receives non-exclusive use license for specific project
- [ ] License scope: construction of building at [address] only
- [ ] Right to use plans is conditional on full payment of fees
- [ ] Any reuse of plans for other projects requires separate written agreement and fee
- [ ] Client must credit architect's name on building plaques, publications, promotional materials
- [ ] Modifications to the design during or after construction require architect's written consent
- [ ] After completion, architect may publish photos and drawings for portfolio use

## Swiss vs. EU Copyright — Key Difference

Switzerland's URG is largely aligned with EU directives but as a non-EU member, Switzerland is not bound by them. The 2020 revision modernized digital provisions. There is **no registration system** in Switzerland — protection is fully automatic.
