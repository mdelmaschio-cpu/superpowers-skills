# /swiss-real-estate-transaction — Swiss Real Estate Transaction Law

## Legal Basis

- **OR art. 216–221**: Real estate purchase contract (Grundstückkaufvertrag)
- **OR art. 216 para. 1**: Mandatory notarial form for real estate purchase
- **ZGB art. 655–657**: Definition of real property and transfer modes
- **ZGB art. 963–964**: Grundbuch inscription as constitutive element of transfer
- **Cantonal law**: Handänderungssteuer, notarial fees, Grundstückgewinnsteuer

---

## 1. Mandatory Notarial Form (OR art. 216)

### Requirement
Every contract for the **sale, purchase, or promise to buy** real estate must be:
1. In **written form authenticated by a notary** (öffentliche Beurkundung)
2. Signed before the notary by both parties (or authorized representatives)

Consequence of non-compliance: the contract is **null and void** (nichtig) — no exception, no ratification possible.

### What Requires Notarial Form
- Final purchase contract (Kaufvertrag)
- Preliminary contract (Vorvertrag — OR art. 216 para. 2): also requires notarial form if it creates an obligation to buy/sell
- Option agreements (Kaufrecht/Kaufsoption)
- Building right contracts (Baurechtsvertrag — ZGB art. 779)
- Constitution of Stockwerkeigentum (condominium)

### What Does NOT Require Notarial Form
- Letter of intent / term sheet (unilateral, non-binding)
- Reservation agreement with deposit (Reservationsvertrag) — if drafted carefully as non-binding, but courts look at substance over label
- Mandate to search for a buyer (Mäklervertrag — OR art. 412)

**Architect's risk**: never draft a document that could be construed as a preliminary purchase contract without involving a notary. Even informal written agreements committing parties to buy/sell land may be challenged as either binding (requiring notarial form) or void.

## 2. Two Cantonal Notarial Systems

Switzerland has two fundamentally different notarial systems:

| System | Cantons | Who authenticates |
|--------|---------|------------------|
| **Latin notariat** (notaio di diritto latino) | TI, VS, GE, NE, JU, FR, VD | Independent notary — certifies identity and legal content; advises both parties neutrally |
| **Germanic system** (Urkundsperson/Official) | ZH, BE, LU, AG, SG, GR, BS, BL, SZ, ZG and others | Cantonal official (Grundbuchamt employee or cantonal notary) — records the deed, less advisory role |

### Practical Difference
- **Latin cantons (e.g., Ticino)**: notary reads the entire deed aloud, ensures parties understand, has advisory duty to both. Often same notary handles deed + Grundbuch formalities.
- **Germanic cantons (e.g., Zurich)**: authentication is more procedural; parties are expected to have their own lawyers. Separate authentication office and Grundbuch are common.

## 3. Due Diligence Before Purchase

### Step 1 — Grundbuch Extract
Request certified extract (beglaubigter Grundbuchauszug) from cantonal Grundbuchamt:
- Owner identity and legal basis of ownership
- All Dienstbarkeiten (easements) — may restrict use or height
- Grundlasten (real burdens) — recurring obligations running with the land
- Grundpfandrechte (mortgages) — must be discharged or assumed at closing
- Baurecht (building right) — check if land is encumbered with existing Baurecht

### Step 2 — ÖREB/RDPPF Cadastre
*Öffentlich-rechtliche Eigentumsbeschränkungen* — public law restrictions NOT shown in the Grundbuch:

| Restriction type | Source |
|-----------------|--------|
| Building zone / zoning plan (Zonenplan) | Communal/cantonal |
| Building line (Baulinie) | Cantonal building authority |
| Environmental protection zones | BAFU/federal |
| Contaminated sites (Altlasten) | Cantonal environmental authority |
| Forest borders (Waldabstandslinien) | Cantonal forest authority |
| Monument protection | Cantonal heritage authority |
| Road reserve (Strassenlinien) | Cantonal/municipal |

Access: national portal at oereb.geo.admin.ch (most cantons); some cantonal portals (TI: map.geo.ti.ch)

### Step 3 — Contaminated Sites (Altlasten)
Check federal Altlasten cadastre (cadastre des sites pollués, altlasten.admin.ch). A contaminated site may require remediation at owner's cost — can be a condition precedent to purchase.

### Step 4 — Building Permits History
Request from communal building authority:
- Existing permits and whether works were completed as permitted
- Outstanding violations (Wiederherstellungsverfügungen)
- Historic uses that may create contamination liability

## 4. Transaction Structure

### Phase 1 — Reservation / Letter of Intent
- Non-binding letter of intent (Term Sheet) or Reservationsvertrag
- Deposit (Réservation): CHF 1'000–5'000 — refundable if transaction doesn't proceed
- **Critical**: ensure document is drafted as non-binding; a binding preliminary contract requires notarial form

### Phase 2 — Due Diligence Period
- Typically 2–4 weeks for residential; 4–8 weeks for commercial/development sites
- Commission: structural survey, environmental assessment, legal due diligence (ÖREB, Grundbuch), valuation

### Phase 3 — Notarial Deed (Kaufvertrag)
Content of the deed:
```
1. Parties (seller, buyer, authorized representatives)
2. Property description (parcel numbers, area, Grundbuch references)
3. Purchase price and payment terms
4. Closing date (Übergabedatum)
5. Mortgage assumptions or discharge conditions
6. Transfer of utilities, ancillary costs, property taxes (pro-rata)
7. Representations and warranties (Gewährleistungen)
8. Condition precedents (e.g., financing, building permit)
9. Penalties for non-performance
10. Notary's certification
```

### Phase 4 — Grundbuch Inscription
- Notary submits deed to Grundbuchamt
- **Title passes at inscription** — not at deed signing
- Timeline: same day to 2 weeks depending on canton workload
- Provisional entry (Vormerkung) protects buyer's position between deed and inscription

## 5. Taxes and Costs

### Handänderungssteuer (Transfer Tax)
Cantonal tax on change of ownership. Rate varies significantly:

| Canton | Rate |
|--------|------|
| Zurich (ZH) | 0% (abolished 1995 for most transactions) |
| Bern (BE) | 1.8% |
| Ticino (TI) | 2.0% |
| Valais (VS) | 2.0% |
| Geneva (GE) | 3.0% |
| Vaud (VD) | 2.2% |
| Basel-City (BS) | 3.0% |

*Usually shared 50/50 between buyer and seller unless agreed otherwise.*

### Grundstückgewinnsteuer (Capital Gains Tax on Real Property)
- Cantonal tax on the **gain** (selling price minus acquisition cost + improvements)
- Progressive rate based on gain amount and **holding period** — longer holding = lower rate
- Exemption for **replacement of primary residence** if proceeds reinvested within 2 years
- Seller's tax — buyer not liable (but may be registered as guarantor in some cantons)

### Notary Fees
- Regulated by cantonal tariff
- Typically 0.1–0.5% of transaction value
- Grundbuch inscription fees: CHF 500–2'000+ depending on transaction complexity

### Mortgage Discharge Costs
- Grundbuchamt fee to cancel existing Schuldbrief or mortgage: CHF 300–1'000
- If paper Schuldbrief: cancellation (Kraftloserklärung) required if lost

## 6. Off-Plan Sales (Verkauf auf Plan) — Developer/PPE

### Legal Structure
Developer sells unbuilt PPE units before or during construction:
- **Vorvertrag** (preliminary contract): notarial form required — commits both parties to sign final deed at completion
- **Werklieferungsvertrag** (supply + works contract): hybrid of sale + construction — courts apply mixed contract rules; some courts require notarial form
- SIA 118 terms often incorporated for the construction component

### Staggered Payment Structures
Standard in Swiss development:
```
10% at Vorvertrag
20% at foundation completion
20% at shell (Rohbau) completion
20% at building enclosure
20% at interior fit-out
10% at final acceptance
```
Each payment stage should be certified by the project architect (Bauleiter) in writing.

### Buyer Protections in Off-Plan
- Bank guarantee for advance payments (Vorauszahlungsgarantie) — strongly recommended
- Notarial Vorvertrag with penalty clause for developer non-performance
- Right of inspection before final payment
- Defect acceptance protocol at handover

## Checklist — Real Estate Due Diligence for Architects' Clients

- [ ] Certified Grundbuch extract (all 5 sections)
- [ ] ÖREB/RDPPF cadastre extract
- [ ] Contaminated sites (Altlasten) check
- [ ] Building permit history from communal authority
- [ ] Zoning compliance: confirm proposed use is permitted in zone
- [ ] Easements mapped and impact on design assessed
- [ ] Existing Grundpfandrechte: discharge plan confirmed with seller's bank
- [ ] Handänderungssteuer rate confirmed (which canton)
- [ ] Grundstückgewinnsteuer: seller has confirmed liability (buyer not responsible)
- [ ] Notary instructed and deed date agreed
- [ ] Financing confirmation from buyer's bank (Finanzierungsbestätigung)
