# /building-permit-ch — Swiss Building Permit Process

Guide to the cantonal building permit procedure in Switzerland (Baubewilligung / Permis de construire / Licenza edilizia).

## Key Principle: Cantonal Competence

Building law is **cantonal in Switzerland** — each of the 26 cantons has its own building act (Baugesetz/Loi sur les constructions). The federal framework (RPG/LAT) sets land use planning principles; cantons implement them.

## Standard Procedure

### 1. Pre-Consultation (Voranfrage / Préavis)
- Optional but strongly recommended for complex projects
- Submit concept plans and program to the municipal building authority
- Receive informal feedback before full application
- Not legally binding, but establishes dialogue
- Timeline: 4–8 weeks

### 2. Application Submission (Baugesuch / Demande de permis)
**Typical documents required:**
- Site plan (Situationsplan) at 1:500 or 1:1000
- Floor plans, sections, elevations at 1:100
- Façade materials and colors
- Volume/surface calculations (GV, GF, HNF per SIA 416)
- Energy proof (Energienachweis/Justificatif énergétique) per cantonal SIA 380/1 requirements
- Shadow study if relevant (Verschattungsstudie)
- Environmental impact assessment if required (UVP/EIE)
- Accessibility compliance statement (SIA 500 / nLHin)
- Structural report summary (for major works)
- Owner consent if applicant ≠ owner

### 3. Publication (Publikation / Publication)
- Application published in cantonal/municipal gazette
- **Objection period**: typically 30 days
- Neighbors and third parties may file objections (Einsprache)

### 4. Specialist Consultations (Mitberichte)
Municipality consults specialist authorities:
- Cantonal monument protection (Denkmalpflege) if historic area
- Cantonal fire protection authority (Gebäudeversicherung)
- Road authority for access
- Cantonal energy office
- Water protection if near watercourses

### 5. Decision (Baubewilligung / Permis de construire)
- Granted with or without conditions
- Conditions (Auflagen) must be fulfilled before/during construction
- Valid typically 2–3 years; can be extended once
- **Appeal period**: 20–30 days after decision

## Objections (Einsprachen)

- Any person with legitimate interest may object during publication period
- Grounds: breach of zoning rules, impact on neighbors, environmental concerns
- Dispute resolution: mediation → cantonal administrative court → Federal Supreme Court
- **Practical advice**: early neighbor consultation prevents formal objections

## Post-Permit Requirements

- **Baubeginnmeldung**: notify authority before starting construction
- **Rohbauabnahme**: inspection of structural shell (canton-dependent)
- **Schlussabnahme / Fertigstellungsmeldung**: final inspection/notification at completion
- Obligation to notify deviations from approved plans during construction
