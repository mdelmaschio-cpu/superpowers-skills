# /sia-118-contract — SIA 118 Construction Contract

Reference guide for Swiss construction contracts under SIA 118 (Allgemeine Bedingungen für Bauarbeiten) and the underlying Swiss Code of Obligations (CO art. 363–379).

## SIA 118 vs CO — Hierarchy

- CO art. 363–379: statutory provisions for contracts for work (Werkvertrag) — **mandatory floor**
- SIA 118: detailed standard conditions — apply when contractually incorporated
- Special conditions (Besondere Bedingungen): project-specific additions — override SIA 118 where inconsistent

**Always verify**: SIA 118 must be explicitly incorporated by reference in the contract.

## Key Contract Elements

### Scope of Work (Leistungsbeschrieb)
- References the Leistungsverzeichnis (LV) / bill of quantities
- Unit rates (Einheitspreise) or lump sum (Pauschalpreis)
- Lump sum: contractor bears quantity risk; changes only for genuine scope change
- Unit price: client bears quantity risk; measurement-based payment

### Price and Payment
- Partial payments (Akontorechnungen): monthly, against certified progress
- **Retention (Einbehalt)**: typically 10% of each invoice, capped at 5% of contract value at substantial completion
- Retention release: 50% at Abnahme, 50% at end of defect period
- Final account (Schlussrechnung): submitted by contractor, reviewed/certified by architect
- **Interest on late payment**: per CO, 5% p.a. after 30 days

### Contractor's Obligations
- Execute work according to contract, rules of the art (Regeln der Baukunde), and applicable standards
- Notify architect immediately of unforeseen site conditions (SIA 118 §52)
- Not to subcontract without client consent (SIA 118 §26)
- Maintain insurance: third-party liability, all-risk construction insurance (ARGE)

### Client's Obligations
- Provide site access
- Make payments on time
- Cooperate with architect's instructions
- Conduct Abnahme within reasonable time of notification

## Defect Liability

### During Construction
- Defects must be remedied before Abnahme or at client's election

### After Abnahme (SIA 118 §180 / CO art. 371)

| Defect type | Limitation period |
|-------------|------------------|
| Apparent defects (known at Abnahme) | Must be reserved in Abnahmeprotokoll or lost |
| Hidden defects (SIA 118) | **2 years** from Abnahme |
| Construction works (CO art. 371 §3) | **5 years** from Abnahme (non-waivable) |
| Fraudulently concealed defects | 10 years |

**Practical**: always note SIA 118 §180 (2-year) AND CO art. 371 (5-year) in the contract — the longer CO period protects the client and cannot be contractually shortened.

### Remedies for Defects (CO art. 368)
1. **Remedy** (Nachbesserung): contractor repairs at own cost
2. **Price reduction** (Minderung): if remedy refused or impossible
3. **Rescission** (Wandelung): only if defect is fundamental
4. **Damages**: if contractor at fault

## Change Orders (Nachträge) — SIA 118 §58–64

- **Client-ordered changes**: client has right to order changes; contractor must price within 30 days
- **Contractor-initiated extras**: only valid if architect approved in writing before execution
- **Pricing of extras**: based on LV unit rates where applicable; otherwise cost + margin
- **Extensions of time**: must be claimed simultaneously with the Nachtrag

## Force Majeure and Unforeseen Conditions

- Unforeseen ground conditions: contractor must notify immediately (SIA 118 §52); entitles additional compensation
- Force majeure (höhere Gewalt): time extension without additional compensation
- **COVID / disruption clauses**: negotiate specifically for material price escalation if market volatile

## Dispute Resolution (SIA 118 §§96–98)

1. Negotiation between parties
2. **Expert determination** (Experte): neutral expert for technical disputes; decision binding unless challenged
3. **Mediation**: recommended before litigation
4. **Ordinary courts**: cantonal courts, then Federal Supreme Court
5. **Arbitration**: possible by agreement; SIA arbitration rules available

## Red Flags in Construction Contracts

- Lump sum with vague scope → risk of disputes on what's included
- No Abnahme procedure defined → uncertain start of defect period
- Retention clause missing → no security for defects
- No force majeure / escalation clause → contractor bears all risk
- Subcontracting without restriction → quality control lost
- Payment terms >30 days → cash flow strain on contractor
